    ✶ Motion Graphic, Visual Identity, Spatial Experience ✶

    This motion poster series is made as a visual identity for a mock event for Hito Steyerl’s essay “In Defense of the Poor Image.” The essay emphasizes the cultural importance of images/videos with poor quality, especially in a day and age where low quality equates to low relevance.

    Taking inspiration on low quality and the contrast with its significance, this poster series can be divided into 5 individual moving posters that spell out the name of the essay and author. Collectively, the background forms a message “the poor cinema,” which is an important concept throughout the essay. The background is a pixelated render of Steyerl’s well known film, “November.”
