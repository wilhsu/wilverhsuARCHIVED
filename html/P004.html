WilliamHsu

is a designer based in New York City. Entering his fourth year in Parsons School of Design majoring in Communication Design, he is interested in exploring visual graphics in more forms than one.

His works oftentimes aim to incite internal conversations within the audience, implementing satire and humor in his works. If you’re thinking he is crazy, he is not (depends who’s asking), he just likes to have fun in his work!

    ✶ Poster Design, Branding, Social Campaign ✶

    This visual identity is developed as a mock social campaign for Fashion Revolution Week, which is an informative event in hopes of raising awareness for fast fashion and educating more individuals on this issue. The identity for this campaign consists of bold neon colors, condensed type, and recognizable textures.

    The phrases implemented in this campaign are satirical questions that are meant to pair with the bright-colored identity to captivate audience and increase engagement. In addition to this, the tone of these posters are purposefully humorous on a very serious issue as a gateway for more individuals to engage with these posters.
